Boba Ordering Station Storyboarding and Accessibility Adventure!

Mission Objective


Oh no! Accessolotls have discovered Earth! We all knew it would happen eventually but we're all still terrified. But, behold! They offer to make us a deal. They explain that they will refrain from destroying our planet if we provide them sufficient resources to understand how our most coveted resource, Boba tea, can be ordered without any human interaction. Naturally, we take them up on this offer, promising to provide as many ways as possible for them to step into our shoes and understand the glories, and hardships, of our contactless boba ordering interface.


The Interface


The Contactless Boba Tea ordering station allows boba shop customers to order their favorite drinks without having to speak to another human. The station has a menu where customers can select which drinks they would like, along with options to customize those drinks and the ability to pay directly at the station using a credit card scanner.

The Interview Questions

This set of questions was created to determine the motives and challenges in the boba tea ordering process.

  1. Tell me your story- what brings you to this fine boba serving establishment?
  2. What was your first reaction as you entered the drink selection screen?
  3. Is there anything you would change about the drink ordering menu or customization screens?
  4. Paint me a detailed picture of your thought process as you were selecting and customizing your order.
  5. Imagine that you had to instruct an extraterrestrial being on how to use this ordering device in order to end total world destruction. Is there anything unclear about using this device that you would warn them about or clarify?

The Interview Findings

The observations below were gathered from unsuspecting Boba tea shop customers.

  • The Boba serving establishment was packed with groups of stressed out college students and families, and those who chose to order contactlessly chose to in order to avoid longer wait times to order in person.
  • Both new and returning customers were overwhelmed and confused by the number of different drinks, drink categories, and customization options, noting that some drinks appeared in multiple different categories.
  • All customers struggled to determine which category their drink was under, and none utilized the search bar.
  • The customization options available were not the same across all drinks, and all customers noted that it was unclear which customization options were required to be selected and which toppings automatically came with each drink.
  • New customers wished there were instructions on how to order drinks using the station, and notibly had trouble finding the "add to cart" and "checkout" buttons.

Personas


Below are persona descriptions created from the interview responses in order to create a better understanding of the human users of this ordering station.

Persona 1: Annika

  • Annika is a college student who routinely gets boba with her friends to treat herself after a stressful day of studying.
  • Annika has trouble finding her usual drink as the categories and drink organization doesn't make sense to her.
  • She experiences the stress and confusion of trying to find her go-to drink among all of the different drinks and categories, representing the overworked college students who regularly get boba.




Persona 2: Wren

  • Wren has never gotten Boba before but wanted to try it as a special treat with her partner and kids after a long week of work.
  • Due to the lack drink details, she has no idea what drink she would like. She is further stressed by the lack of instructions on how to customize her drink, and ultimately ends up getting something random.
  • She experiences the overwelmth and confusion with learning the new interface in the high pressure atmosphere surrounded by her kids and other customers, representing the families who want to order Boba contactlessly.

Accessolotl Analysis


An accessibility analysis of the boba ordering station was conducted in order to determine how usable it was to beings with disabilities. Everyone deserves to Access-o-lotl!

  • The ordering station has no audio output and therefore must be accessed visually, making it impossible to use if you have limited or no vision.
  • There are no options to increase the font size of the text elements, making the ordering station extremely hard to use if you have low vision.
  • There are no instructions on how to use the ordering station, and the customization options are not consistent across drinks.
  • The ordering station is placed around eye level for a 5 foot tall person and the height is not adjustable, making it impossible to use if someone uses a wheelchair.
